Improving Federal Customer Experience

Overview: President Biden directed federal agencies to put people first by improving the public's experience with government services. The order aims to make federal services more customer-centric.

Bottom Line: This order will not require significant action from most higher education institutions.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Federal Research Grants: Interactions with federal grant-making agencies may become more user-friendly over time.

Student Financial Aid: Processes for FAFSA and federal student loans could be streamlined.

University Hospital Systems: Interfaces with CMS, VA, and other agencies may be modernized.

Affected Departments:

Financial Aid Office, Research Administration, University Hospital Leadership

Action Plan:

Action Required: No. Aside from general awareness, no proactive steps are needed from most institutions at this time.

Immediate Steps (0-30 days):

  • Review order and inform relevant offices listed above.

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Monitor any communications from affected federal agencies on planned service changes.

Long-term Considerations:

  • Consider user experience improvements in institution's own customer-facing services.

Resource Requirements:

Personnel: None

Budget: None

Technology: None

External Expertise: None
