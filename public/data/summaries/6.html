New Iran Sanctions for Yale

Overview: The US government expanded economic sanctions against Iran to include more industries, which may impact some Yale operations.

Key Impacts on Yale:

  • Restrictions on transactions with Iranian construction, mining, manufacturing, and textile companies
  • Potential compliance requirements for Yale's procurement and international partnerships
  • Possible disruptions to Yale's Iran-related research or academic collaborations

Action Needed: Yes

Important Dates: 01/10/2020: Executive Order 13902 issued

Affected Departments: Procurement and Purchasing, Office of Research Administration, Office of International Affairs, General Counsel

What Changed

The executive order expanded existing US economic sanctions to cover additional sectors of the Iranian economy, including construction, mining, manufacturing, and textiles.

Why Yale Cares

Yale has procurement, research, and international partnerships that may be affected by these new restrictions on doing business with Iran.

Action Steps

  • Review procurement processes to ensure compliance with new Iran sanctions
  • Identify and assess any Iran-related research, academic, or business activities at Yale
  • Consult with the Office of General Counsel on compliance obligations and risk mitigation

Resource Needs

Potential need for additional staff time and legal expertise to review compliance
