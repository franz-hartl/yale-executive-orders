New Digital Assets Regulations

Overview: The President issued an order to regulate cryptocurrencies and other digital assets to protect consumers and prevent financial crimes.

Key Impacts on Yale:

  • Yale may need to update policies and procedures for accepting digital assets and crypto payments
  • Yale's research and innovation efforts around blockchain and cryptocurrency could be affected
  • Yale's finance and IT teams may need to closely monitor new regulatory developments

Action Needed: Yes

Important Dates: N/A

Affected Departments: Finance, Information Technology, Research and Innovation

What Changed

The order establishes a national policy for digital assets and directs agencies to ensure consumer protection, financial stability, and mitigation of illicit finance risks.

Why Yale Cares

As a leading research university, Yale is actively involved in the development of digital assets and blockchain technology. The new regulations could impact Yale's financial operations and innovative research in this space.

Action Steps

  • Review current policies and procedures for accepting digital assets and crypto payments
  • Monitor regulatory developments and updates from relevant agencies
  • Assess the impact on Yale's research and innovation efforts in blockchain and cryptocurrency

Resource Needs

Potential need for additional staff time and expertise to stay compliant with new regulations
