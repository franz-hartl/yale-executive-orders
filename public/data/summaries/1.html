Executive Order on U.S. Digital Asset Policy

Overview: Establishes national policy on digital assets including cryptocurrencies. Directs agencies to ensure consumer protection, financial stability, and legal compliance.

Bottom Line: Universities with digital asset exposure should evaluate legal and financial risks.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Endowment Management: Review digital asset investments for policy alignment

Institutional Finance: Assess banking relationships and payment systems

Research Activities: Ensure grant-funded crypto research complies with policy

Important Dates:

  • Reports from agencies due various dates in late 2022

Affected Departments:

Endowment Office, VP Finance, Sponsored Projects Office

Action Plan:

Action Required: Yes, if the university has digital asset exposure or related activities

Immediate Steps (0-30 days):

  • Inventory university digital asset exposure
  • Review policy with legal counsel and financial advisors

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Assess digital asset risks and value alignment
  • Develop compliance plan for affected activities

Long-term Considerations:

  • Monitor evolving digital asset regulations
  • Adapt investment and financial strategies

Resource Requirements:

Personnel: Additional compliance staff time

Budget: Potential for increased operating costs

Technology: Reporting system updates

External Expertise: Legal and financial advisory support
