Bribery Law Enforcement Paused

Overview: This executive order temporarily halts enforcement of the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act, which prohibits bribing foreign officials. The goal is boosting American economic and national security interests.

Bottom Line: Universities may see more foreign funding opportunities but face increased financial misconduct risks.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Foreign Partnerships: Potential for increased investment and collaboration with foreign entities

Financial Compliance: Heightened risk of bribery and need for robust internal controls

Reputation Management: Public perceptions around ethics and integrity may suffer

Important Dates:

  • 02/14/2025: Order issued
  • 03/01/2025: Recommend anti-corruption policy review

Affected Departments:

Finance, Research Administration, Legal/Compliance

Action Plan:

Action Required: Yes. Evaluate foreign funding relationships and strengthen anti-corruption safeguards.

Immediate Steps (0-30 days):

  • Inform leadership and key personnel
  • Assess current foreign funding sources
  • Review anti-bribery policies and training

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Implement financial reporting changes
  • Update employee anti-corruption training

Long-term Considerations:

  • Advocate for clear regulatory guidance
  • Monitor long-term financial and reputational impacts

Resource Requirements:

Personnel: Possible increased compliance staffing

Budget: Potential for more foreign funding

Technology: May need enhanced accounting systems

External Expertise: Outside counsel on FCPA
