Removing Barriers to U.S. Leadership in AI

Overview: This executive order seeks to bolster U.S. leadership in artificial intelligence by eliminating barriers and increasing federal investment in AI research, development, and education. It emphasizes the federal government's role in prioritizing AI advancement.

Bottom Line: Universities should prepare for potential increases in AI research funding along with intensified competition for resources.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Research Funding: Potential increase in federal funding for AI research

Educational Programs: Expansion of AI-related educational offerings may be needed

Resource Competition: Heightened competition among universities for AI resources and talent

Affected Departments:

Research Administration, Academic Departments, Government Relations

Action Plan:

Action Required: Yes - Universities should assess their current AI capabilities and strategically position themselves for funding opportunities.

Immediate Steps (0-30 days):

  • Review current AI research portfolio and identify expansion areas
  • Assess AI educational offerings and determine growth opportunities
  • Engage with government relations to understand funding prospects

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Develop strategic plan for AI research and education initiatives
  • Build partnerships with other institutions and industry leaders

Long-term Considerations:

  • Long-term sustainability of AI investments
  • Balancing AI with other institutional priorities

Resource Requirements:

Personnel: Additional AI faculty and research staff may be needed

Budget: Increased budget allocation for AI programs and infrastructure

Technology: Expansion of computing power and data storage for AI

External Expertise: Guidance on federal funding processes and partnerships
