White House Re-establishes Faith-Based Partnership Office

Overview: Executive Order 14018 re-establishes the White House Office of Faith-Based and Neighborhood Partnerships. The office aims to coordinate efforts to engage faith-based and community organizations in addressing economic and social challenges.

Bottom Line: Higher education institutions should monitor potential partnership opportunities through this office, especially related to economic development initiatives.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Community Engagement: May provide avenues for universities to partner with local faith-based and community organizations on initiatives.

Economic Development: Could align university economic development efforts with faith-based programs, especially in underserved areas.

Public-Private Partnerships: May open doors for joint projects between universities, government agencies, and community groups.

Affected Departments:

Community Engagement, Economic Development, Government Relations

Action Plan:

Action Required: No immediate action required, but administrators should stay apprised of developments from this office that could impact university strategies and opportunities.

Immediate Steps (0-30 days):

  • Designate a point person to monitor news and announcements from the re-established office.
  • Review existing community and faith-based partnerships to identify potential alignment.

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Assess university priorities and initiatives that could be enhanced through faith-based collaborations.
  • Provide relevant updates to senior leadership and trustees as the office's agenda takes shape.

Long-term Considerations:

  • Look for opportunities to provide input on the office's priorities to advance higher education goals.
  • Evaluate how partnerships with faith-based organizations fit into the university's long-term community engagement strategy.

Resource Requirements:

Personnel: No new personnel needed, but may require some staff time to monitor and assess relevant developments.

Budget: No immediate budget impact.

Technology: No new technology requirements.

External Expertise: May opt to consult with peer institutions or associations on engagement strategies.
