Energy Dominance Order May Impact University Sustainability Efforts

Overview: The National Energy Dominance Council will develop a strategy to maximize U.S. fossil fuel production and exports. Federal agencies must prioritize policies supporting fossil fuel expansion.

Bottom Line: The order's emphasis on fossil fuels may increase energy costs and hinder university sustainability initiatives.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Energy Costs: Potential increases in electricity and heating expenses

Research Funding: Possible reduction in federal grants for clean energy research

Sustainability Goals: Challenges meeting carbon reduction targets and green building standards

Important Dates:

  • 06/30/2025: Council strategy recommendations due

Affected Departments:

Facilities Management, Finance/Budget Office, Sponsored Research Office

Action Plan:

Action Required: Yes, assess budget impacts and advocate for continued sustainability funding.

Immediate Steps (0-30 days):

  • Analyze potential energy cost increases
  • Identify at-risk clean energy research grants
  • Quantify impact on sustainability goals

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Develop FY26 budget contingency plans
  • Engage with researchers on alternate funding

Long-term Considerations:

  • Revisit strategic plans for carbon neutrality
  • Build coalition to advocate for balanced energy policy

Resource Requirements:

Personnel: Potential need for energy/sustainability analyst

Budget: 5-10% increase in utility budget, reallocation of research funding

Technology: Enhanced energy monitoring and control systems

External Expertise: Energy policy consultants, grant writing support
