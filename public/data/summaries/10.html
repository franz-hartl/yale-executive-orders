Streamlining Regulations for Federal Agencies

Overview: This executive order encourages independent regulatory agencies to improve how they develop and review regulations.

Key Impacts on Yale:

  • Provides guidance on regulatory review processes for Yale's administrative compliance efforts
  • Encourages more transparent and efficient regulation development by federal agencies that impact Yale
  • May lead to changes in how Yale interacts with federal regulators on compliance matters

Action Needed: Yes

Important Dates: 07/11/2011: Executive Order signed by President Obama

Affected Departments: Office of the General Counsel, Office of Compliance, Department of Finance and Administration

What Changed

The executive order asks independent federal agencies to follow principles for improving how they create and review regulations.

Why Yale Cares

As a university, Yale must comply with many federal regulations. This order could impact how Yale interacts with and responds to those regulatory agencies.

Action Steps

  • Review the executive order and identify any potential impacts on Yale's compliance efforts
  • Coordinate with relevant departments to understand changes in federal agency processes
  • Update policies and procedures as needed to adapt to new regulatory review approaches

Resource Needs

Staff time to analyze the order, communicate with federal agencies, and revise compliance programs as necessary
