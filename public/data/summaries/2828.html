Reviewing Immigration Policies to Reduce Illegal Immigration Funding

Overview: Executive Order 14113 directs federal agencies to review their immigration-related policies, guidance, and practices. The goal is to identify and eliminate any federal funding that directly or indirectly supports illegal immigration.

Bottom Line: Higher education institutions should assess whether any of their federal funding could be at risk due to direct or indirect support of illegal immigration.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Federal Grants: Review federal grants to ensure compliance with immigration-related eligibility requirements.

Financial Aid: Confirm student aid is not inadvertently supporting students engaged in illegal immigration.

Research Funding: Evaluate research funding sources and projects for any immigration-related risks.

Affected Departments:

Government Affairs, Financial Aid Office, Research Administration, Legal Counsel

Action Plan:

Action Required: Yes, institutions should proactively review policies and funding for potential immigration compliance gaps.

Immediate Steps (0-30 days):

  • Review the institution's policies related to verifying immigration status.
  • Identify federal funding streams with immigration-related eligibility criteria.
  • Meet with affected departments to coordinate an immigration compliance review.

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Audit federal grants, financial aid, and research funding for immigration risks.
  • Develop an action plan to remediate any identified compliance gaps.

Long-term Considerations:

  • Monitor evolving federal immigration policies for impact on the institution.
  • Provide immigration compliance training to relevant personnel.

Resource Requirements:

Personnel: Staff time to conduct immigration compliance review and remediation.

Budget: Potential need for outside immigration counsel. Possible reduction in federal aid/funding.

Technology: Systems to track/verify immigration status may need enhancement.

External Expertise: Immigration legal counsel to assist with compliance review.
