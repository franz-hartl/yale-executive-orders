Biden Orders Whole-of-Government Approach to Climate Crisis

Overview: Executive Order 14008 elevates the climate crisis as a central consideration in U.S. foreign policy and national security. It directs a coordinated approach across the federal government to combat climate change.

Bottom Line: Higher education institutions should prepare for increased focus on climate change research, education, and operations.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Research: Potential increased funding for climate and clean energy research

Education: Opportunity to expand climate change curriculum and programs

Operations: Pressure to reduce carbon footprint and improve sustainability

Important Dates:

  • Ongoing: Monitor implementation and funding opportunities

Affected Departments:

Research Administration, Academic Affairs, Facilities Management

Action Plan:

Action Required: Yes, institutions should review the order and identify areas of alignment or needed changes.

Immediate Steps (0-30 days):

  • Read executive order and share with relevant departments
  • Identify current climate research and education activities
  • Assess campus sustainability efforts

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Convene working group to coordinate campus response
  • Communicate climate priorities to faculty, students and staff

Long-term Considerations:

  • Align strategic plans with federal climate priorities
  • Build capacity in climate research and academic programs

Resource Requirements:

Personnel: Potential need for sustainability staff and faculty hires

Budget: Opportunity to secure research funding, possible green technology costs

Technology: May require updated emissions tracking and reporting

External Expertise: Leverage climate policy and technology partners
