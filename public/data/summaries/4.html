Biden Executive Order on Climate Crisis

Overview: The order directs the federal government to address climate change risks and invest in clean energy.

Key Impacts on Yale:

  • Requires Yale to assess and disclose climate-related financial risks
  • Encourages Yale to shift investments toward clean energy and away from fossil fuels
  • May lead to new federal funding opportunities for climate-related research and projects

Action Needed: Yes

Important Dates: No specific dates provided

Affected Departments: Office of Sustainability, Office of Investments, Office of Grants and Contracts, Office of General Counsel

What Changed

The order requires federal agencies, including those that provide funding to universities, to prioritize climate change in their policies and spending.

Why Yale Cares

As a major research university, Yale needs to understand and mitigate the financial and operational risks of climate change. The order also provides opportunities for climate-related funding and collaboration.

Action Steps

  • Assess climate-related financial risks and develop a plan to disclose and address them
  • Review investment portfolio and shift toward sustainable, clean energy options
  • Identify and pursue new federal funding opportunities for climate research and projects

Resource Needs

Additional staff time and expertise in areas like climate risk analysis, sustainable investing, and grant writing
