Measuring Climate Financial Risk

Overview: Executive Order 14030 directs federal agencies to develop strategies to measure, disclose, and mitigate climate-related financial risks to government programs, assets, and liabilities. The goal is to increase the government's resilience against climate change impacts.

Bottom Line: Higher ed institutions should evaluate how climate risks may impact their finances, investments, and federal funding.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Financial management: Universities may need to assess and disclose climate-related financial risks.

Research funding: Climate risk considerations could affect federal research grants and contracts.

Infrastructure planning: Institutions may need to account for climate risks in facilities and infrastructure investments.

Affected Departments:

Finance and Administration, Facilities Management, Sponsored Research Office

Action Plan:

Action Required: Not immediately. Monitor for further guidance from federal agencies and funding bodies.

Immediate Steps (0-30 days):

  • Designate responsibility for tracking implementation to a senior administrator
  • Brief leadership on potential implications for the institution

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Evaluate current climate risk disclosure practices
  • Identify relevant federal funding streams that may incorporate climate risk

Long-term Considerations:

  • Develop an institutional climate risk management strategy
  • Integrate climate risk into strategic and financial planning

Resource Requirements:

Personnel: May require dedicated staff to assess and manage climate risks

Budget: Potential for increased costs to mitigate climate risks

Technology: May need climate risk modeling and disclosure tools

External Expertise: Likely need for specialized climate risk consultants
