New Iran Sanctions Impacting Yale

Overview: This executive order imposes additional sanctions on Iran's energy and financial sectors.

Key Impacts on Yale:

  • Increased regulatory compliance requirements for Yale's international activities and transactions
  • Potential impacts on Yale's research and collaborative projects involving Iran
  • Possible restrictions on payments, purchases, or other financial dealings with Iranian entities

Action Needed: Yes

Important Dates: 07/30/2012: Executive Order 13622 signed by President Obama

Affected Departments: Office of General Counsel, Office of Sponsored Projects, Office of International Affairs, Procurement Services, Accounting Services

What Changed

The executive order expands sanctions on Iran's energy and petrochemical sectors and strengthens financial restrictions.

Why Yale Cares

These sanctions could impact Yale's international activities, research collaborations, and financial transactions involving Iran or Iranian entities.

Action Steps

  • Review existing and planned activities, projects, and financial dealings to identify potential issues
  • Consult with the Office of General Counsel to ensure compliance with the new sanctions
  • Coordinate with the Office of Sponsored Projects to address any impacts on research or other sponsored projects
  • Update procurement and accounting processes to prevent prohibited transactions

Resource Needs

Additional staff time for compliance review, policy updates, and coordination across affected departments
