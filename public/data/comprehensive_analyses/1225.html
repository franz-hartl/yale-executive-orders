Executive Order 2025-02928: Implications for Higher Education Sustainability Efforts

Overview: EO 2025-02928 establishes the National Energy Dominance Council to develop a federal strategy maximizing U.S. fossil fuel production and exports. Agencies are directed to prioritize policies supporting the expansion of fossil fuel extraction and infrastructure. While aiming to bolster U.S. energy independence and geopolitical power, the order's strong tilt toward fossil fuels poses challenges for higher education institutions in managing energy costs, advancing clean energy research, and achieving sustainability goals.

Bottom Line: The order's redirection of federal resources toward fossil fuels may strain university budgets, slow progress on carbon reduction, and diminish competitiveness in the evolving clean energy economy.

Policy Context:

Policy Background:

The order follows the 2017 U.S. withdrawal from the Paris Agreement and the 2022 Green New Deal initiative. It represents a pendulum swing back toward Trump-era energy policies.

Legal Framework:

Directs federal agencies to use authority under the Energy Policy Act, Clean Air Act, and Outer Continental Shelf Lands Act to support fossil fuel development. Vulnerable to court challenges on climate impact and administrative procedure grounds.

Higher Education Context:

Diverges from the higher education sector's increasingly aggressive commitments to carbon neutrality, fossil fuel divestment, and clean energy workforce development. May place public institutions at odds with state-level climate policies.
