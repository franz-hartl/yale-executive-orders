Executive Order 13622 - Additional Iran Sanctions and Implications for Higher Education

Overview: President Obama issued Executive Order 13622 on July 30, 2012 authorizing additional sanctions with respect to Iran. The order targets Iran's energy and petrochemical sectors, prohibiting certain financial and material support to Iran. It also expands existing financial sanctions. While significant for U.S. foreign policy, the order does not appear to create new compliance obligations for colleges and universities.

Bottom Line: This executive order primarily concerns trade and investment restrictions on Iran's energy, petrochemical, and financial sectors. There are no evident new requirements that would impact core university functions of education, research, or campus operations. The U.S. has progressively expanded sanctions on Iran since the Iran Sanctions Act of 1996, targeting Iran's energy sector and nuclear program. This order builds on Iran sanctions in the Comprehensive Iran Sanctions, Accountability, and Divestment Act of 2010 and other executive orders.

The President issued this order under the authority of the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA), the National Emergencies Act (NEA), and section 301 of title 3, United States Code. It is part of the broader U.S. sanctions regime against Iran.
