Biden Orders Review to Reduce Federal Bureaucracy

Overview: President Biden issued an executive order directing federal agencies to identify positions, offices, and programs that are unnecessary or duplicative. The goal is to reduce the size and cost of the federal bureaucracy.

Bottom Line: The impact on higher education institutions is currently unclear and will depend on the specific cuts proposed.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Research Funding: Potential reductions in available federal grants if research programs/offices are cut

Student Aid: Possible changes to federal financial aid programs if Dept. of Education is affected

Regulatory Compliance: Reduced regulatory burden if duplicative oversight functions are eliminated

Affected Departments:

Sponsored Projects Office, Financial Aid Office, Research Administration

Action Plan:

Action Required: No, until specific cuts are proposed that directly impact higher education institutions

Immediate Steps (0-30 days):

  • Monitor for further details on proposed cuts and their potential impact

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Assess exposure to programs/grants at risk of being cut
  • Identify alternative funding sources

Long-term Considerations:

  • Re-evaluate budgets and strategic priorities based on funding landscape changes
