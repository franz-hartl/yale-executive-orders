Terrorist Designations for Criminal Groups May Impact University Partnerships

Overview: This executive order enables the government to designate international criminal organizations as terrorists. This imposes sanctions on the groups and anyone materially supporting them.

Bottom Line: Universities must carefully review international funding and partnerships for any potential connections to sanctioned groups to ensure compliance.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Research Funding: Research grants or contracts from entities later designated under this order could be frozen or rescinded.

International Partnerships: Partnerships with foreign institutions or groups must be vetted to avoid inadvertent association with sanctioned entities.

Regulatory Compliance: Failure to comply with sanctions under this order could lead to significant financial and legal penalties.

Important Dates:

  • 01/29/2025: Executive order issued

Affected Departments:

Research Administration, Global Partnerships, General Counsel, Government Affairs

Action Plan:

Action Required: Yes - universities should proactively assess foreign funding sources and partnerships in light of this order.

Immediate Steps (0-30 days):

  • Brief senior leadership on potential impacts
  • Initiate review of foreign funding sources and partnerships
  • Consult external experts on terrorist finance regulations

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Strengthen due diligence processes for foreign engagements
  • Develop contingency plans for potential loss of funding

Long-term Considerations:

  • Monitor implementation of the order and any designations made
  • Engage with policymakers to provide feedback on higher ed impacts

Resource Requirements:

Personnel: Potential increased workload for compliance and legal staff

Budget: Possible need for additional due diligence on foreign funding

Technology: May require enhanced vetting software/databases

External Expertise: Specialized guidance on terrorist finance laws
