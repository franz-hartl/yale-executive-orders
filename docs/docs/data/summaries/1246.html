Order Requires Universities to Rename Entities Deemed Unaligned with American Greatness

Overview: This executive order directs federal agencies to evaluate references to individuals or events and rename those not representing American greatness. The goal is to better reflect the country's values and achievements.

Bottom Line: Universities must review and potentially rename buildings, programs and other entities, which may involve significant costs and resources.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Naming Conventions: Universities will need to review names of buildings, programs, professorships, etc.

Community Engagement: Renaming decisions will require consulting with students, faculty, alumni and other stakeholders.

Budget and Resources: Institutions may face unexpected costs for legal reviews, signage changes, and other renaming logistics.

Important Dates:

  • 06/30/2025: Recommended completion of initial naming reviews

Affected Departments:

President's Office, General Counsel, Advancement/Development, Facilities Management

Action Plan:

Action Required: Yes. Universities should immediately begin assessing campus names against the order's criteria.

Immediate Steps (0-30 days):

  • Establish cross-functional task force to lead naming review
  • Inventory all named entities on campus
  • Determine criteria for renaming based on order's guidance

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Conduct legal and historical research on potentially problematic names
  • Engage stakeholders for input on renaming candidates

Long-term Considerations:

  • Develop ongoing process for evaluating future naming proposals
  • Consider proactive renaming where appropriate to reflect institutional values

Resource Requirements:

Personnel: Task force members, legal counsel, historians, community engagement staff

Budget: Costs for signage, legal fees, ceremonies, and PR/communications

Technology: Research databases, surveying tools for stakeholder input

External Expertise: Historians, DE&I consultants, government relations advisors
