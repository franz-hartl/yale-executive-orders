Deregulation Order May Impact University Costs and Operations

Overview: This executive order seeks to spur economic growth by eliminating burdensome regulations on businesses and industries. While the full financial impact is unclear, reduced regulatory requirements could lead to cost savings for universities.

Bottom Line: Administrators should proactively assess potential budget, safety and environmental regulation changes.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Budgets: Reduced compliance costs in some areas but possible unintended budget impacts

Safety: Potential changes to safety regulations may require operational adjustments

Environment: Deregulation may affect environmental compliance obligations and costs

Important Dates:

  • TBD: Deadlines depend on specific agency rulemaking timelines

Affected Departments:

Finance, Environmental Health & Safety, Facilities, Research Administration

Action Plan:

Action Required: Yes - Regulatory affairs and finance teams should analyze order specifics against current university practices.

Immediate Steps (0-30 days):

  • Identify university activities currently subject to relevant federal regulations
  • Estimate potential compliance cost savings from reduced regulatory burden

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Adjust budgets to account for estimated regulatory impacts
  • Assess any safety and environmental compliance changes

Long-term Considerations:

  • Monitor SHEEO and industry sources for ongoing deregulatory impacts
  • Factor deregulation into strategic plans around costs and operations

Resource Requirements:

Personnel: Requires cross-functional regulatory working group

Budget: Potential for net savings, but reallocation may be needed

Technology: Minimal new technology anticipated

External Expertise: Possible need for specialized regulatory consulting
