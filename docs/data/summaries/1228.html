Sanctions on ICC Officials Investigating U.S. Personnel

Overview: This executive order imposes sanctions on International Criminal Court officials involved in investigating U.S. personnel. It prohibits U.S. citizens from engaging in transactions with these officials and freezes their U.S. assets.

Bottom Line: While direct financial impact on universities is low, research collaborations or programs involving sanctioned ICC officials could be disrupted.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Research Collaborations: Joint research programs with ICC officials under investigation may need to be suspended.

International Programs: Educational or exchange programs involving affected ICC personnel could be impacted.

Visiting Scholars: Plans to host or employ sanctioned ICC officials as visiting scholars may require alteration.

Affected Departments:

Research Administration, International Programs, Legal Counsel

Action Plan:

Action Required: No, unless university has specific programs or assets involving sanctioned ICC officials.

Immediate Steps (0-30 days):

  • Identify any research collaborations, international programs, or visiting scholar plans involving ICC.
  • Consult with university counsel on appropriate actions regarding identified programs or individuals.

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Communicate with affected departments and personnel regarding the sanctions.
  • Develop guidelines and approval processes for collaborations and programs involving foreign officials.

Long-term Considerations:

  • Monitor updates to sanctions list and assess impact on university programs.
  • Foster alternative collaboration opportunities to maintain global engagement.

Resource Requirements:

Personnel: No major changes, potential need for coordination across departments

Budget: Minimal unless specific programs are affected

Technology: Not applicable

External Expertise: Consult with outside legal counsel for guidance on complex cases
