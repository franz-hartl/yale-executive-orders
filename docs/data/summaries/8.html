Executive Order on Regulating U.S. Financial System

Overview: Sets core principles to guide regulation of the U.S. financial system. Directs Treasury Secretary to review existing regulations for alignment with principles.

Bottom Line: No direct regulatory changes for higher ed, but could influence future policy.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Endowment Management: Principles emphasizing free market and limiting regulation could affect endowment investment options and strategies long-term.

Student Loans: Reduced regulation of private student loans could expand options but decrease consumer protections.

Institutional Finances: Changes to financial regulations and economic conditions could impact borrowing, liquidity, and financial stability.

Affected Departments:

Investment Office, Financial Aid, Finance/Treasury

Action Plan:

Action Required: No. Monitor for potential long-term impacts.

  • Inform relevant offices of the order.
  • Note core principles in financial planning.

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Monitor Treasury Department regulatory reviews and findings.

Long-term Considerations:

  • Consider engaging in policy discussions on financial regulation.
  • Evaluate any regulatory changes for institutional impacts.
