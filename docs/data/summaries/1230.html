Transgender Athlete Ban in Women's Sports

Overview: This executive order prohibits transgender women from participating in women's collegiate and high school sports. It claims their inclusion unfairly disadvantages cisgender women athletes.

Bottom Line: Universities must ban transgender women athletes or risk losing federal funding.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Athletics: Women's sports teams cannot include transgender athletes

Compliance: New policies and procedures needed to enforce the ban

Legal: Potential lawsuits challenging the ban on discrimination grounds

Important Dates:

  • TBD: Deadline to implement compliant policies

Affected Departments:

Athletics, Legal/General Counsel, Diversity & Inclusion, Title IX Office

Action Plan:

Action Required: Yes, to avoid legal exposure and maintain federal funding

Immediate Steps (0-30 days):

  • Review current roster of women athletes to identify transgender individuals
  • Consult legal counsel on ban implementation and discrimination risks
  • Draft new athletics eligibility policies barring transgender women

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Establish a process to verify sex assigned at birth for women athletes
  • Communicate new policies to athletics staff, coaches and student athletes

Long-term Considerations:

  • Monitor legal challenges to the order and similar state laws
  • Assess ongoing Title IX compliance and equal opportunity impact

Resource Requirements:

Personnel: Potential need for outside legal counsel

Budget: Legal fees to defend against potential lawsuits

Technology: Sex verification tools and databases

External Expertise: Expert policy guidance from NCAA and other associations
