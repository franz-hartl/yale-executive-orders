New Iran Sanctions Unlikely to Affect Universities

Overview: President Obama issued an executive order imposing new sanctions on Iran's energy, petrochemical, and financial sectors in response to Iran's nuclear program. The order expands existing sanctions but does not appear to introduce any new requirements directly relevant to higher education institutions.

Bottom Line: While universities should review the details, this order is unlikely to require any immediate changes to current practices or operations for most institutions.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Finances: No expected impact on university financing, endowments, tuition, or other core funding

Research: Unlikely to affect federal research grants or international research partnerships

Enrollment: Does not change student visa policies or introduce new restrictions on Iranian student enrollment

Important Dates:

  • 07/30/2012: Executive order issued

Affected Departments:

Finance Office, Office of Sponsored Projects, International Programs Office

Action Plan:

Action Required: No, unless the institution has significant investments or business ties in Iran's energy, petrochemical or financial sectors, which is unlikely.

Immediate Steps (0-30 days):

  • Review order to confirm no impact to current practices

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Monitor any clarifying guidance from Department of Education or other agencies

Long-term Considerations:

  • Stay informed of any future changes to Iran sanctions that may be more relevant to higher education

Resource Requirements:

Personnel: No additional personnel needed

Budget: No budget impact

Technology: No new technology required

External Expertise: Unlikely to require outside legal or subject matter expertise
