Executive Order Mandates University Promotion of Christianity

Overview: This executive order aims to address perceived anti-Christian bias in higher education. It requires universities to establish policies and programs that promote Christian beliefs and practices on campus.

Bottom Line: Universities must take immediate action to ensure compliance with the Christianity promotion requirements.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Resource Allocation: Universities may need to divert funds from other priorities to implement required Christian programs.

Legal Compliance: Institutions will incur costs to ensure adherence to the new requirements.

Campus Climate: Mandated Christian promotion may affect diversity, equity and inclusion efforts.

Important Dates:

  • Specific implementation deadlines will be communicated to universities.

Affected Departments:

Office of the President/Chancellor, Provost's Office, Diversity & Inclusion Office, General Counsel, Finance & Administration

Action Plan:

Action Required: Yes, universities must implement policies and programs to promote Christianity on campus.

Immediate Steps (0-30 days):

  • Inform key stakeholders and form a cross-functional working group.
  • Assess current religious/spiritual life policies and identify gaps.
  • Consult with legal counsel on compliance requirements.

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Develop an implementation plan with milestones and resourcing needs.
  • Begin drafting new policies and program outlines.

Long-term Considerations:

  • Monitor ongoing compliance and incorporate into institutional DEI goals.
  • Evaluate impact on campus spiritual climate and interfaith relations.

Resource Requirements:

Personnel: May require new hires or reallocation of existing staff.

Budget: Likely requires dedicated implementation budget and ongoing funding.

Technology: Potential changes to course management and administrative systems.

External Expertise: Outside legal counsel to assess compliance issues. Consultants on Christian programming.
