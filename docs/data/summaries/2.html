Mandatory Climate Risk Reporting for Yale

Overview: The President ordered federal agencies to develop ways for companies to measure, disclose, and reduce climate-related financial risks.

Key Impacts on Yale:

  • Yale must report on climate risks in its financial disclosures
  • Yale may need to change operations to reduce climate-related financial risks
  • Yale's investments and endowment could be impacted by new climate risk rules
  • Increased reporting requirements for Yale's sustainability and finance teams

Action Needed: Yes

Important Dates: No specific dates provided

Affected Departments: Office of Sustainability, Office of Investment Management, Office of Budget and Financial Planning, Office of General Counsel

What Changed

The executive order directs federal agencies to develop new rules and guidance for companies to disclose and mitigate climate-related financial risks.

Why Yale Cares

As a major university with a large endowment and complex financial operations, Yale will be impacted by new mandatory climate risk reporting and mitigation requirements.

Action Steps

  • Assess Yale's current climate risk exposure and reporting practices
  • Develop a plan to comply with forthcoming federal climate risk disclosure rules
  • Evaluate investment and operational strategies to reduce climate-related financial risks

Resource Needs

Additional staff time and expertise in sustainability, finance, and legal compliance to implement new climate risk management processes
