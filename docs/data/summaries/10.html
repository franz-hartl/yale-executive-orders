Improving Regulation and Review at Independent Agencies

Overview: This executive order encourages independent regulatory agencies to follow principles for improving regulation and regulatory review. The goal is to promote better analysis, coordination, and public participation in the rulemaking process.

Bottom Line: University administrators should monitor potential compliance changes from financial regulatory agencies.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Administrative Burden: Could increase compliance requirements and administrative workload

Rulemaking Process: Encourages more thorough analysis and public input in agency rulemaking

Cross-agency Coordination: Promotes greater coordination between independent regulatory agencies

Affected Departments:

Finance, Sponsored Projects, Research Administration

Action Plan:

Action Required: No immediate action required, but continued monitoring recommended

Immediate Steps (0-30 days):

  • Review order and identify potentially affected areas

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Monitor rulemaking activities of relevant agencies
  • Assess potential compliance and administrative impacts

Long-term Considerations:

  • Engage in public comment opportunities for relevant proposed rules

Resource Requirements:

Personnel: No additional personnel needed

Budget: No direct budget impact

Technology: No new technology requirements

External Expertise: May require specialized compliance expertise long-term
