Executive Order Targets Deregulation

Overview: President Biden has directed federal agencies to review significant regulations and identify those that are unlawful, ineffective, or overly burdensome. The goal is to streamline federal regulations and reduce compliance burden.

Bottom Line: Higher education institutions should prepare for potential changes to federal regulations and compliance requirements.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Administrative burden: Reduced compliance and reporting requirements in areas deemed over-regulated

Shifting landscape: Possible major policy changes in areas like Title IX, accreditation, financial aid

Uncertainty: Lack of clarity on which regulations will be targeted and on what timeline

Affected Departments:

Financial Aid, Registrar / Student Records, Research Administration, Accreditation & Compliance

Action Plan:

Action Required: Not immediately - this launches agency reviews but does not directly change any existing requirements yet.

Immediate Steps (0-30 days):

  • Inform key administrators of the executive order
  • Identify a point person to monitor agency announcements
  • Review institutional practices related to major regulations

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Analyze potential impact of regulations being reconsidered
  • Provide input to agencies on how changes would affect institutions

Long-term Considerations:

  • Adapt compliance infrastructure for new regulatory regime
  • Advocate for regulations that provide stability and support institutional missions

Resource Requirements:

Personnel: Possible reduced compliance staff if regulations are streamlined

Budget: Potential for reduced compliance costs

Technology: Updates to reporting/tracking systems

External Expertise: Insight on agency priorities and timelines
