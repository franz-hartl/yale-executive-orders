Executive Order on Protecting Against Invasion

Overview: This executive order aims to protect against foreign threats. It may require changes to university administrative procedures for compliance.

Bottom Line: Universities must assess order's impact on operations and be prepared to adapt policies.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Administrative Policy: May require updates to procedures and documentation

Financial Operations: Potential changes to accounting and reporting

International Programs: Could affect student/scholar visa processing and services

Important Dates:

  • TBD: Effective date of order and any compliance deadlines

Affected Departments:

Central Administration, Finance/Controller, International Office

Action Plan:

Action Required: Yes, universities will likely need to take action to align with new requirements.

Immediate Steps (0-30 days):

  • Analyze order text to identify specific impacts
  • Coordinate with campus stakeholders
  • Develop action plan for compliance

Short-term Actions (1-3 months):

  • Implement necessary policy and process changes
  • Train staff on new requirements

Long-term Considerations:

  • Monitor for additional federal guidance
  • Assess ongoing impact to operations

Resource Requirements:

Personnel: May require additional staff time to implement changes

Budget: Potential for increased administrative costs

Technology: Updates to software/databases may be needed

External Expertise: Legal counsel review of compliance obligations
