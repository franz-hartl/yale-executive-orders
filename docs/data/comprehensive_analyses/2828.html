Assessing Federal Funding Streams for Immigration Compliance Risks

Overview: Executive Order 14113, titled 'Ending Taxpayer Subsidization of Open Borders', initiates a broad federal review of immigration-related policies, guidance, procedures and practices. The stated intention is to identify and eliminate any sources of federal funding that are directly or indirectly supporting illegal immigration. For higher education institutions, this means assessing whether any federal aid, grants, or research dollars could be at risk due to insufficient immigration compliance controls.

Bottom Line: Higher education institutions need to proactively audit their federal funding streams and related compliance practices to surface and address any potential issues regarding improper support of illegal immigration. Both direct funding and pass-through funding are in scope.

Policy Context:

Policy Background:

U.S. immigration enforcement has been contentious across recent administrations. This EO escalates matters by tying immigration concerns to federal funding, which is a critical revenue stream for many colleges and universities.

Legal Framework:

Various statutes restrict federal public benefits to U.S. citizens or certain legal immigrants (e.g. 8 USC §§1611, 1621). But application to higher education has had limited enforcement to date.

Higher Education Context:

Higher education is under mounting scrutiny and financial pressure on multiple fronts. Losing access to federal funding over immigration issues would be devastating for many institutions.

Key Impacts on Higher Education:

Federal Student Aid: Review eligibility criteria, verification procedures, and disbursement practices for federal financial aid to ensure only legal recipients.

Research Grants: Assess research personnel, partnerships, and funding sources for immigration-related risks and proper oversight.

Other Grants/Contracts: Systematically audit all other federal funding agreements to identify any with immigration status requirements for participation.

Enrollment Management: Admissions, registration and student support processes should be evaluated for sufficient immigration compliance and documentation.

Sponsored Programs: Build immigration compliance reviews into processes for pursuing federal funding opportunities and managing awarded projects.

Affected Departments:

Financial Aid Office, Research Administration, Sponsored Programs Office, Enrollment Management, Government Affairs, Legal Counsel, Administration & Finance

Implementation Details:

Compliance Requirements:

Leverage existing Form I-9 and E-Verify employment eligibility verification processes as well as SEVIS tracking for international students. Ensure consistent processes and documentation of legal immigration status across all areas related to federal funding eligibility.

Technical Analysis:

Federal funding agreements likely to require certifications of institutional immigration compliance, so underlying procedures and systems must be sufficiently sophisticated and deterministic to support such certifications.

Cross-Functional Impacts:

An immigration compliance review regarding federal funds will necessarily touch many areas and require extensive cross-departmental cooperation to be effective. Upfront coordination is critical.

Risk Analysis:

Significant compliance risk from lack of consistent, verifiable immigration procedures for federal fund recipients. But overcorrecting also poses risks (e.g. national origin discrimination). Expert guidance is essential.
