Overview: This executive order seeks to address claimed anti-Christian bias within U.S. higher education institutions. It mandates that universities enact policies and programs which actively promote Christian beliefs and practices on campus. Institutions must take prompt action to establish implementation plans.

Bottom Line: Universities nationwide will need to dedicate significant financial, personnel and administrative resources to meet the order's compliance requirements, potentially impacting other strategic priorities and altering the campus spiritual climate.

Policy Context:

Policy Background:

The order does not provide evidence of systemic anti-Christian bias in higher education. It represents an unprecedented federal intervention into universities' ability to manage their own religious and spiritual affairs.

Legal Framework:

The order's constitutionality is questionable given the First Amendment's prohibition on state establishment of religion. It may violate universities' freedoms of speech and association. Legislation could be required for full implementation and enforcement.

Higher Education Context:

U.S. higher education is facing growing religiously-motivated regulations which may impinge on institutional autonomy and academic freedom. This order accelerates the erosion of separation of church and state within the sector.
