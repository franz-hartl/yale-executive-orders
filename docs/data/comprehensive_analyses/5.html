Restricting U.S. Investment in Chinese Military Companies

Overview: Prohibits all U.S. persons from purchasing or holding publicly traded securities (or derivatives) of companies identified as "Communist Chinese military companies." Requires divestment of existing positions within 365 days. Expansive definition of covered companies supporting China's military-industrial complex.

Bottom Line: Universities must thoroughly review endowment exposures, divest prohibited holdings by November 11, 2021, and consider broader implications for research and global engagement with China.

Policy Background:

Reflects escalating U.S.-China tensions and concerns over China's military-civil fusion. Builds on prior restrictions on telecom giant Huawei and others. First formal investment ban.

Legal Framework:

Relies on International Emergency Economic Powers Act and National Emergencies Act. Definition of "Communist Chinese military company" derives from Section 1237 of National Defense Authorization Act.
