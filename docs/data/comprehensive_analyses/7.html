U.S. Reinstates Extensive Economic Sanctions Against Iran

Overview: Following the U.S. withdrawal from the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA) in May 2018, this executive order comprehensively reinstated the U.S. sanctions regime against Iran that had been waived or lifted under the JCPOA. The sanctions target Iran's energy, shipping, shipbuilding, financial, insurance, and other sectors and penalize foreign companies and financial institutions that do business with Iran.

The JCPOA, agreed to in 2015 between Iran and the P5+1 nations, had lifted many of the sanctions on Iran in exchange for restrictions on Iran's nuclear program. The U.S. withdrawal from the deal and the snapback of sanctions represents a major shift in U.S. policy toward Iran.
